About

Biography

Khaled Al Moulla was born and raised in northern Sweden to Lebanese parents, spending most of his upbringing in Örnsköldsvik. He later moved southward to study physics and astronomy at Uppsala University. For his bachelor's thesis project, Khaled investigated the magnetosphere of Saturn using data from the Cassini spacecraft, whereas during the master's studies he transitioned to observational astrophysics, focusing on stellar spectra and the characterization of exoplanet host stars.

Since 2020, Khaled is a PhD student at the University of Geneva in Switzerland, and a member of the National Centre of Competence in Research (NCCR) PlanetS community.

In his free time, Khaled enjoys going to the cinema, hiking in the Swiss alps, and writing short stories.

Research Interests

  • High-resolution spectroscopy
  • Sun-as-a-star observations
  • Solar and stellar activity
  • Exoplanet detections
  • Extreme Precision in Radial Velocity (EPRV)
